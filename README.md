# Horizontal World

**The sky is empty. But the ground is full.**  
A living book about worlds that do not need a ceiling to hold them up.

## What this is

Many traditions assume meaning, value, and the sacred must come from *above* — a creator, heaven, final judgment, or escape from ordinary life. When that vertical anchor is removed, nihilism often rushes in.

This book explores **horizontal worlds** of meaning. It draws especially from East Asian traditions (Confucianism, Daoism, Chan/Zen) while also examining parallel immanent approaches elsewhere. Here, meaning grows sideways — through relationships, rituals, social roles, nature, daily practice, and attentive engagement with ordinary life.

> **Meaning does not descend from the sky; it grows sideways from the ground we share.**

The project examines both the promise and the real shadows of such worlds.
