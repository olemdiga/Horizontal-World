# Horizontal World

This is a notebook about worlds that don't need a ceiling to hold them up.**

---

## What this is

Many traditions and philosophies assume that meaning, value, and the sacred must come from *above* – a creator, a heaven, a final judgment, an escape from ordinary life. Remove that vertical anchor, and nihilism often rushes in.

But other traditions – especially in East Asia – never built that ceiling. They found meaning **horizontally**: in relationships, rituals, nature, daily work, and the immanent flow of life. No sky required. The ground is enough.

This notebook collects notes, essays, and think pieces exploring horizontal worlds: what they are, how they work, and why they matter – especially for those who have lost the vertical but still want the ground to be full.
