# A Horizontal Reading List

This is a living garden of books, essays, poems, and films that illuminate horizontal worlds — works that find meaning through immanence, relationships, ritual, the sacred ordinary, cultivation, harmony, and sideways existence rather than transcendence or vertical escape.

The list is not a canon. Wander freely.

---

## Classical East Asian Foundations

- **Confucius – *The Analects* (Lunyu)**  
  The root text of role ethics, ritual (*li*), and social harmony without a creator god or afterlife drama.

- **Laozi – *Daodejing***  
  The foundational expression of the Dao that flows left and right, *wu wei*, and non-forcing action.

- **Zhuangzi – *The Zhuangzi***  
  Radical horizontality. Dissolves hierarchies between human/insect, dream/reality, useful/useless. Playful wisdom at its best.

- **Dōgen – *Shōbōgenzō* (Treasury of the True Dharma Eye)**  
  Zen master on “practice-enlightenment” and the everyday mind as the Way. “Chopping wood, carrying water.”

- **Huainanzi**  
  Han dynasty synthesis of Daoist, Confucian, and Legalist thought — a rich horizontal cosmology of self-organizing processes.

---

## Modern East Asian & Daoist-Influenced Works

- **Kakuzo Okakura – *The Book of Tea***  
  Elegant meditation on tea ceremony as horizontal ritual and aesthetic of simplicity.

- **D.T. Suzuki – *Zen and Japanese Culture***  
  Explores Zen in tea, gardens, haiku, and everyday arts.

- **Ursula K. Le Guin – *The Lathe of Heaven*** (and her translation of the *Daodejing*)  
  Daoist-infused fiction exploring harmony, unintended consequences, and the dangers of forcing the world.

---

## Indigenous & African Traditions

- **Robin Wall Kimmerer – *Braiding Sweetgrass***  
  Indigenous science and ceremony: reciprocity with the living world and seeing nature as kin.

- **John S. Mbiti – *African Religions and Philosophy***  
  Foundational text on ancestor veneration, communal ethics, and immanent African cosmologies.

- **David Abram – *The Spell of the Sensuous***  
  Phenomenology that recovers embodied, horizontal perception of the more-than-human world.

---

## Western & Modern Horizontal Voices

- **Henry David Thoreau – *Walden***  
  Deliberate living and deep attention to nature and ordinary experience.

- **Gary Snyder – *The Practice of the Wild* and *Turtle Island***  
  Zen, ecology, and bioregionalism — work, walking, and wildness as practice.

- **Mary Oliver – *Devotions*** (selected poems)  
  Luminous attention to the natural world and the body.

- **Jenny Odell – *How to Do Nothing***  
  Reclaiming horizontal presence and attention in the digital age.

- **Matthew Crawford – *Shop Class as Soulcraft***  
  The meaning and dignity found in skilled manual work.

---

## Philosophy & Critique

- **Friedrich Nietzsche – *The Gay Science* and *Thus Spoke Zarathustra***  
  Diagnoses the European death of God and warns against both vertical remnants and shallow horizontality.

- **Charles Taylor – *A Secular Age*** (especially on the immanent frame)  
  Essential analysis of how meaning is possible after the vertical collapses.

- **Alasdair MacIntyre – *After Virtue***  
  Recovery of virtue ethics through practices and living traditions.

- **François Jullien – *The Propensity of Things***  
  Deep study of Chinese *shi* (horizontal efficacy and situational power).

- **Hans-Georg Moeller – *The Philosophy of the Daodejing***  
  Clear, modern interpretation of Laozi’s radical immanence.

- **James C. Scott – *The Art of Not Being Governed***  
  Horizontal politics in practice: societies that actively resist vertical state structures.

---

## Fiction & Film

- **Kazuo Ishiguro – *The Remains of the Day***  
  Quiet exploration of duty, role, regret, and horizontal dignity.

- **Sayaka Murata – *Convenience Store Woman***  
  Darkly insightful look at modern Japanese social conformity and role pressure.

- **Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter… and Spring** (Kim Ki-duk, 2003)  
  Cyclical time, ritual, and nature as teacher.

- **Paterson** (Jim Jarmusch, 2016)  
  A bus driver’s quiet, repetitive, poetic life — pure horizontality.

- **Still Walking** (Hirokazu Kore-eda, 2008)  
  Family, generational continuity, and meaning in ordinary meals and conversations.

- **My Neighbor Totoro** (Hayao Miyazaki, 1988)  
  Gentle animism — the sacred lives in trees, rain, and everyday wonder.

---

## How to Use This List

Read sideways. Let one work lead naturally to another. Some entries illuminate the promise of horizontal worlds; others reveal their shadows and tensions.

The list will grow over time. Contributions and suggestions are welcome.

*The ground is full.*
